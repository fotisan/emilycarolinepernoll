A calm space that’s also practical.

Many people arrive feeling on edge, burned out, or disconnected from themselves or others. Sometimes it’s a clear event. Often it’s the accumulation. My job is to help you notice the “blind spots” that keep repeating, and to support real change that holds up outside the therapy room.

My style is engaged, collaborative, and integrative. We can include somatic awareness, relational patterns, cognitive tools, and skill-building. Mindfulness and mind-body practices can help you understand both current stress and the older stories your nervous system still carries.

Services

Choose a starting point. We can adjust as we learn what helps you most.

Individual therapy

For anxiety, overwhelm, depression, self-esteem, trauma responses, and patterns that feel hard to break. We work with both insight and skills so you can feel steadier day-to-day.

Couples therapy

For communication breakdown, conflict loops, emotional disconnection, and rebuilding after a rupture. We slow the cycle, strengthen repair, and create a clearer path forward.

Family therapy

FAQ

Short answers, so the first step feels simpler.

What happens in the free 15-minute consultation?
We’ll briefly discuss what’s bringing you in, what you’re hoping for, and whether I’m the right fit. You can ask practical questions about format, scheduling, and next steps.
Do you offer in-person and online therapy?
Yes. Sessions are available both in-person in Santa Monica and online, depending on your needs and preferences.
How long is a typical session?
Most sessions are scheduled in a standard therapy length. If you need a different format, we can discuss options.
What issues do you commonly help with?
Common areas include anxiety, grief, addiction patterns, relationship conflict, infidelity recovery, trauma responses, and life transitions. If you’re unsure, start with the consult.
Do you work with couples and families?
Yes. I see individuals, couples, and families. We’ll choose an approach that matches your goals and the dynamics involved.
Do you accept insurance?
If you have out-of-network benefits, you may be able to seek reimbursement. You’re welcome to ask what documentation you’d need.
